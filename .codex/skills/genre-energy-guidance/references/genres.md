# Genre Energy Guidance — Reference Data

Per-genre, per-role energy guidance for jam agents.
Each role gets three energy bands: LOW (1-3), MID (4-6), HIGH (7-10).
Guidance is descriptive musical intent — agents realize it through their sound palettes.

## Dark Ambient
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Sparse distant hits, single muted kick or rim tap per bar. Ghost-like.
- MID (4-6): Slow pulse with filtered hats and occasional tom rolls. Cavernous reverb.
- HIGH (7-10): Industrial pulse, relentless kick with metallic percussion layers. Oppressive density.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Sub drone on root, barely moving. Deep and sustained.
- MID (4-6): Slow root-fifth motion, dark filtered tone. Ominous crawl.
- HIGH (7-10): Distorted low pulse, aggressive rhythmic ostinato. Seismic.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Single held tone or two-note interval, heavy reverb. Spectral.
- MID (4-6): Slow descending phrases, wide intervals. Eerie and hollow.
- HIGH (7-10): Dissonant clusters, rapid short phrases cutting through the murk. Urgent.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Single held dyad or low-mid drone pad with light crackle tail. Sparse but audible harmonic bed.
- MID (4-6): Slow chord swells or dissonant cluster pads, long reverb, occasional metallic texture. Uneasy harmonic fog.
- HIGH (7-10): Layered drones with pulsing chord stabs and harsh texture bursts. Oppressive harmonic wall.

## Pop
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Finger snaps or soft rimshot with light kick. Intimate.
- MID (4-6): Standard kick-snare-hat foundation, clean and punchy. Danceable.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving four-on-the-floor with open hats, crash fills. Anthemic.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Whole notes on roots, warm tone. Supportive and gentle.
- MID (4-6): Root-fifth patterns following chord changes. Bouncy, melodic hooks.
- HIGH (7-10): Syncopated octave runs, active passing tones. Driving energy.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Simple phrase with space, stepwise motion. Singable and gentle.
- MID (4-6): Hook-driven melody, repetitive catchy motifs. Bright and clear.
- HIGH (7-10): Soaring phrases, wide intervals, climactic register shifts. Anthemic.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Warm pad or piano whole-note chords, gentle syncopated stabs. Clear harmonic bed.
- MID (4-6): Bright piano/EP comping outlining the progression with offbeat accents. Hook support.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving chord stabs, wide pads, and octave-doubled attacks. Anthem lift.

## Jazz
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Brushes-feel, ride pattern only, minimal kick. Conversational.
- MID (4-6): Swing ride with walking kick hints. Ghost snare and hat embellishments.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving swing, active kick comping, open hi-hat accents, fill-heavy.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Whole/half note roots. Upright tone, warm and sustained.
- MID (4-6): Walking quarter-note bass, chromatic approach tones. Classic swing.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast walking lines with bebop fills, occasional double-time runs.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Sparse melodic fragments, long tones with vibrato. Ballad phrasing.
- MID (4-6): Swing-eighth melodies, chord-tone arpeggios, blue notes. Conversational.
- HIGH (7-10): Rapid bebop lines, chromatic runs, wide interval leaps. Virtuosic.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse shell voicings with soft comp punctuations. Airy combo support.
- MID (4-6): Swing-aware shell and 4-note voicings, syncopated comp hits. Classic jazz comping.
- HIGH (7-10): Denser comp rhythm, punchy stabs, brief upper extensions. Driving swing/fusion energy.

## Blues
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Slow shuffle kick-snare, laid-back feel. Lazy and swampy.
- MID (4-6): Shuffle groove with ghost snare, steady hat. Roadhouse energy.
- HIGH (7-10): Hard shuffle, driving kick, rim shots and crash accents. Full-tilt boogie.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Slow root notes, half-time feel. Deep and deliberate.
- MID (4-6): Walking blues bass, root-fifth-flat-seventh motion. Classic 12-bar feel.
- HIGH (7-10): Boogie-woogie patterns, busy eighth-note runs on chord tones. Driving.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Bent single notes, long sustain, call-and-response phrasing. Mournful.
- MID (4-6): Pentatonic licks, blue note bends, classic turnaround phrases. Soulful.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast pentatonic runs, double stops, aggressive bends. Fiery.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Slow shuffle chord pads or organ-like swells. Roomy and smoky.
- MID (4-6): Shuffle comping stabs, turnaround hits, piano/guitar rhythm support. Roadhouse glue.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving boogie chord chops, stronger backbeat stabs, lightly overdriven comping. Full-band push.

## Funk
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Stripped pocket groove, kick on one, ghost snares. Tight and minimal.
- MID (4-6): Syncopated kick-snare with open hat on the and. Dirty pocket.
- HIGH (7-10): Complex syncopation, fast hat work, snare flams, multi-layered polyrhythm. Explosive.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root with octave pop, minimal motion. Locked to kick.
- MID (4-6): Slap-pop patterns, syncopated sixteenths with dead notes. Groove-heavy.
- HIGH (7-10): Rapid slap runs, chromatic fills, thumb-and-pop acrobatics. Parliament energy.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Single rhythmic stab, chord tone on the one. Punctuation.
- MID (4-6): Rhythmic horn-like riffs, clav-style repeated motifs. Choppy and percussive.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast wah-style licks, double-time rhythmic runs. Scorching.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Tight muted offbeat stabs, short chord pops. Pocket-defining support.
- MID (4-6): Syncopated sixteenth-note comp chops with clav/piano-like attack. Engine room.
- HIGH (7-10): Aggressive interlocking stabs, rhythmic chord bursts, occasional filter motion. Explosive funk grid.

## Waltz
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Light brush on 1, whisper hats on 2-3. Delicate oom-pah-pah.
- MID (4-6): Clear kick on 1, snare/rim on 2-3, gentle hat throughout. Elegant.
- HIGH (7-10): Strong kick on 1, driving snare on 2-3, cymbal swells on phrase ends. Grand.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root on beat 1 only, sustained. Open waltz foundation.
- MID (4-6): Root on 1, chord tones on 2-3. Classic waltz bass pattern.
- HIGH (7-10): Moving quarter notes across all beats, wide intervallic leaps. Dramatic.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Long phrases across bars, gentle stepwise motion. Wistful.
- MID (4-6): Lilting melodic lines, grace notes and turns. Elegant dance.
- HIGH (7-10): Sweeping phrases, dramatic leaps, orchestral flourishes. Grand ballroom.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Gentle sustained chords on phrase starts, soft pulses on beats 2-3. Ballroom bed.
- MID (4-6): Clear chord pulses supporting the 1-2-3 motion, elegant comping. Dance frame.
- HIGH (7-10): Stronger pushes on beats 2-3 with sweeping harmonic lifts. Grand waltz momentum.

## Afrobeat
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Stripped clave pattern, single shaker, minimal kick. Hypnotic pulse.
- MID (4-6): Full polyrhythmic kit, interlocking kick-snare with shaker and bell. Tony Allen pocket.
- HIGH (7-10): Maximum polyrhythmic density, multiple percussion layers, driving open hats. Relentless.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Pedal tone root, slow rhythmic pulse. Grounding.
- MID (4-6): Syncopated root-octave pattern, locked to kick accents. Fela groove.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast pentatonic runs, aggressive syncopation, pushing the beat. Propulsive.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Call phrase with rests, pentatonic. Simple horn-section feel.
- MID (4-6): Repeated riff patterns, rhythmic horn stabs, pentatonic hooks. Hypnotic.
- HIGH (7-10): Dense layered riffs, rapid call-response, soaring phrases over polyrhythm. Ecstatic.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse interlocking chord stabs, short repeated cells. Hypnotic support.
- MID (4-6): Tight offbeat comping patterns locking with percussion, guitar/keys-like pulse. Afrobeat engine.
- HIGH (7-10): Dense interlocking stabs, repeating chord riffs, light bell/percussion accents. Propulsive wall.

## Lo-fi Hip Hop
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Dusty kick and rim, behind the beat, heavy swing. Head-nod only.
- MID (4-6): Boom-bap kick-snare, vinyl-crackle hats, lazy timing. Chill but present.
- HIGH (7-10): Layered drums, faster hat patterns, crisp snare through the dust. Still laid back.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Sub root with slow filter movement. Warm and hazy.
- MID (4-6): Simple melodic bass, jazz chord tones, mellow fingerstyle feel. Smooth.
- HIGH (7-10): More active melodic bass, walking hints with filter warmth. Busy but chill.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Single piano/kalimba notes with long decay and reverb. Ambient.
- MID (4-6): Jazz-influenced hook fragments or gentle top-line arpeggios. Warm keys.
- HIGH (7-10): Busier melodic lines, Rhodes runs, layered with delay. Full but mellow.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Dusty Rhodes/pad chord bed with slow voicing changes. Cozy glue.
- MID (4-6): Soft jazz chord stabs or lazy comping cells with tape-warm feel. Head-nod harmony.
- HIGH (7-10): Busier chord chops, layered pads, tasteful texture accents. Full but mellow.

## Drum & Bass
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Sparse breakbeat hits, half-time kick-snare suggestion. Atmospheric.
- MID (4-6): Classic two-step breakbeat, snare on 2 and 4, rolling hats. Liquid feel.
- HIGH (7-10): Chopped amen-style breaks, rapid-fire snare rolls, double-time hat flurries. Ferocious.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Deep sub drone, slow filter modulation. Subsonic presence.
- MID (4-6): Reese-style bass wobble, following chord movement. Dark and rolling.
- HIGH (7-10): Aggressive bass stabs, rapid retriggering, distorted growl. Neurofunk energy.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Atmospheric high-note pads or thin top-line fragments, slow arpeggio hints. Ethereal.
- MID (4-6): Synth stabs on chord hits, reverb-drenched melodic hooks. Liquid.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast arpeggios, aggressive synth leads cutting through the break. Intense.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Atmospheric chord pads with long tails, sparse harmonic stabs. Spatial support.
- MID (4-6): Reverb-drenched chord hits at phrase points, occasional filtered texture support. Liquid lift.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast chord stabs or rave-like harmonic punches with controlled noise accents. Intense but clear.

## Reggae
### drums
- LOW (1-3): One-drop kick on 3, ghost rim clicks. Minimal and deep.
- MID (4-6): Classic one-drop with cross-stick on 3, steady closed hat. Roots pocket.
- HIGH (7-10): Busy steppers groove, driving kick on every beat, open hat accents. Dancehall energy.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root note on beat 1, sustained and deep. Dub foundation.
- MID (4-6): Walking roots and fifths, melodic bassline locking with the one-drop. Heavy.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast melodic runs, syncopated sixteenths, reggae bass solo energy. Busy.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Sparse horn-like calls or single note sustains, plenty of space. Dub-air phrasing.
- MID (4-6): Rhythmic hook riffs and short horn-like answers around the skank. Classic roots lead support.
- HIGH (7-10): Active melodic lines over the skank, horn-section-style riffs. Celebratory.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse skank offbeats with deep reverb tails. Spacious roots support.
- MID (4-6): Classic offbeat skank comping, tight chops with dub delay throws. Essential reggae glue.
- HIGH (7-10): Faster skank variations, layered chord chops, dub accents. Sound-system drive.

## Prog Rock
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Odd-meter pulse, subtle kick emphasis on the one. Cerebral.
- MID (4-6): Complex time signature groove, tom patterns, cymbal accents on phrase boundaries. Sophisticated.
- HIGH (7-10): Aggressive fills across the odd meter, rapid double-kick, cymbal-heavy passages. Epic.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Sustained root tones following the meter, slow movement. Atmospheric.
- MID (4-6): Melodic bass following the odd-meter phrase, counterpoint to melody. Intricate.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast unison runs with melody, rapid arpeggiated passages. Virtuosic.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Long sustained tones, sparse intervals in the odd meter. Contemplative.
- MID (4-6): Phrase-length melodies across bar lines, arpeggios through chord changes. Progressive.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast scalar runs, wide leaps, polyrhythmic phrasing against the meter. Shredding.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sustained odd-meter chord beds with subtle movement. Expansive foundation.
- MID (4-6): Odd-meter comping riffs and synth/keys stabs marking phrase boundaries. Progressive engine.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving rhythmic chord figures, stacked harmony hits, hybrid texture layers. Epic momentum.

## Latin
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Son clave hint, single timbale tap, light shaker. Sparse groove.
- MID (4-6): Full clave-based groove, kick-snare with timbale accents and shaker. Salsa pocket.
- HIGH (7-10): Dense polyrhythmic percussion, cascara on timbale, fast cowbell, open-close congas feel. Fire.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root on the one, sustained warmth. Simple tumbao hint.
- MID (4-6): Classic tumbao bass pattern, anticipated root on the and-of-four. Driving.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast montuno bass, rapid anticipation patterns, chromatic fills. High-energy salsa.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Simple melodic call, resting on chord tones. Son-like simplicity.
- MID (4-6): Repeated horn-like riff motifs and syncopated lead figures. Danceable.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast melodic runs, call-and-response, brass-section-style intensity. Descarga energy.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse montuno hints or chord punctuations with light percussion support. Clear clave space.
- MID (4-6): Montuno/guajeo-like repeated chord figures in the midrange. Dance-driving comping.
- HIGH (7-10): Dense interlocking chord patterns, bright stabs, selective percussion accents. Descarga propulsion.

## Mixolydian Rock
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Steady kick-snare, simple hat, classic rock feel. Grounded.
- MID (4-6): Driving rock beat, open hat accents, half-time fills. Arena feel.
- HIGH (7-10): Pounding kick, rapid fills, crash-heavy, double-time hat. Stadium rock.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root notes on strong beats, sustained. Power chord foundation.
- MID (4-6): Root-fifth power patterns, pentatonic fills between chord changes. Riff-driven.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast riff unisons, driving eighth-note runs, aggressive attack. Hard rock.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Simple pentatonic phrase, bluesy bends, sustained notes. Soulful.
- MID (4-6): Guitar-like riffs, mixolydian flavor with flat-seven color. Classic rock hooks.
- HIGH (7-10): Fast pentatonic shred, wide bends, soaring climactic phrases. Guitar hero.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sustained open chords or light rhythm strums with amp-like warmth. Grounded rock bed.
- MID (4-6): Driving rhythm-chord riffs, power-chord support, offbeat pushes. Arena support.
- HIGH (7-10): Aggressive power-chord pulses and wide stacked hits. Wall-of-sound drive.

## Cinematic
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Single distant timpani hit per phrase, or silence. Tension.
- MID (4-6): Epic taiko-style pulse, building toms, militaristic snare rolls. Building.
- HIGH (7-10): Full orchestral percussion barrage, rapid timpani rolls, crash cymbal layers. Climax.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Deep sustained drone on root, ominous. Subterranean.
- MID (4-6): Cello-like moving bass, slow chord arpeggiation. Dramatic underscore.
- HIGH (7-10): Rapid ostinato, aggressive low string patterns. Action-sequence intensity.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Single high sustained note, fragile and exposed. Tension wire.
- MID (4-6): Sweeping string-like phrases, rising motion, emotional arc. Underscore melody.
- HIGH (7-10): Full soaring theme, wide intervals, triumphant or tragic climax. Main title energy.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Quiet harmonic pad bed with slow chord swells and faint texture. Tension support.
- MID (4-6): Rising chord swells, rhythmic ostinato stabs, hybrid texture accents. Suspense builder.
- HIGH (7-10): Massive chord hits, layered harmonic beds, trailer-style texture impacts. Blockbuster lift.

## Bossa Nova
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Brushes on ride only, gentle cross-stick hint. Cafe whisper.
- MID (4-6): Bossa clave on rim, light kick on 1 and 3, shaker ghost. Classic bossa.
- HIGH (7-10): Active brush work, louder kick pattern, opened-up hat and bell accents. Upbeat samba hint.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root whole notes, warm upright tone. Gentle foundation.
- MID (4-6): Classic bossa bass, root and fifth with chromatic approach. Elegant walking.
- HIGH (7-10): Active melodic bass, samba-influenced runs, syncopated fills. Dancing.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Simple intervals, sustained notes with natural vibrato. Intimate.
- MID (4-6): Jobim-style phrases, stepwise motion with chromatic neighbor tones. Breezy.
- HIGH (7-10): Active melodic improvisation, wider intervals, playful rhythmic variation. Bright samba.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Gentle syncopated bossa comping cells with soft voicings. Cafe foundation.
- MID (4-6): Classic bossa chord comping with clear syncopation and air. Essential groove identity.
- HIGH (7-10): Brighter, more active comping with samba-leaning motion and chord flourishes. Dancing lift.

## Punk
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Simple kick-snare, quarter notes, stripped bare. Raw and direct.
- MID (4-6): Fast straight-eighth kick-snare, no frills, driving hats. Three-chord energy.
- HIGH (7-10): Blast-beat speed, wall of snare and kick, crashing cymbals constantly. Pure aggression.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Root notes, eighth-note pulse, distorted tone. Simple power.
- MID (4-6): Fast root-fifth patterns, following chord changes tightly. Driving.
- HIGH (7-10): Rapid eighth-note runs, aggressive slides between roots, maximum attack. Relentless.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Single-note or octave hook stab per bar, raw tone. Statement.
- MID (4-6): Fast chord-tone riffs, shout-chorus melodic hooks. Anthemic and raw.
- HIGH (7-10): Rapid-fire melody, screaming register, intense repetition. Manic energy.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse power-chord hits with raw sustain. Statement support.
- MID (4-6): Fast power-chord pulses, tight rhythm support following changes. Audible impact.
- HIGH (7-10): Relentless chord drive, thick attacks, minimal texture grit. Full punk wall.

## Generic
### drums
- LOW (1-3): Sparse, space-dominant — fewer hits, let silence speak.
- MID (4-6): Core groove — kick/snare foundation with moderate texture.
- HIGH (7-10): Full density — layered kit, active cymbal work, frequent fills.
### bass
- LOW (1-3): Sparse, sustained — root notes, long tones.
- MID (4-6): Core groove — root-fifth motion, moderate activity.
- HIGH (7-10): Full motion — busy passing tones, octave runs.
### melody
- LOW (1-3): Sparse, sustained — few notes, wide intervals.
- MID (4-6): Core melody — stepwise phrases, chord-tone motion.
- HIGH (7-10): Full expression — rapid runs, wide leaps, dense phrasing.
### chords
- LOW (1-3): Sparse harmonic bed — short stabs or sustained pads, clearly audible.
- MID (4-6): Core comping identity — rhythmic chord support with space for the lead.
- HIGH (7-10): Driving chord engine — stronger attacks, denser rhythm, controlled layering.
